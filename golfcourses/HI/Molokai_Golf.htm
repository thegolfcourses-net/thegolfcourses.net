The Hawaiian Islands are extraordinary, the most desirable area for golf in the U.S. The breathtaking Pacific Ocean, volcanoes and other mountains, and lush foliage makes Hawaii a golf destination unlike any other. The biggest Island is the Big Island, which has many golf courses that will astound each golfer who goes there to play the game, while Oahu comes to mind first because it's the most populous and easy to travel to from the mainland or elsewhere in the world. Oahu is the home of Honolulu and the fantastic golf courses nearby. However, if you're into out-of-the-way islands, then Molokai just might be the island for you. Waiting to be discovered is Kaluakoi Golf Course, a treat of a golf course, to say the least!!

Molokai Golf Courses

The Island of Molokai, in Hawaii, only has one course that is bookable for tee times at this time. The Kaluakoi Golf Course is located as part of Molokai Properties, on the North side of the island. It was designed by “Ted” Robinson to take advantage of the Ocean views found off Molokai Island, Hawaii. Five playing holes follow the Pacific coastline. The rest of the course is an island treat with deep ravines, towering cliffs and lush fairways and sandy traps. The price will help it become a peaceful retreat you’ll want to go back to!!!
