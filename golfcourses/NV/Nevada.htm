Golf Courses in Nevada

WildHorse Golf Club, Henderson, NV

Want a diversion from playing at the casinos? Want a pleasant golfing experience any time of year? Nevada has it!!! Las Vegas/Henderson can get expensive so check out the off-season rates, or drive one hour West (Hwy 160) to Pahrump and enjoy a fantastic championship course for $40 or so. Mountain Falls in Pahrump is not even well-known to the locals, so when you get there, tell ‘em you heard about the course at Thegolfcourses.net. If you must play Vegas Golf, enjoy Badlands, a 27-hole championship Johnny Miller layout, the nearby 36 hole Angel Park, designed by Arnold Palmer, or Silverstone, a beautiful 27-hole club in Northwest Las Vegas. Don’t overlook Black Mountain, in Henderson, usually a great bargain for such a good course, another 27 holes of Las Vegas area golf. Or you might want to head out to any of the resorts around the state. Want to get a short distance away for more choices? Try the quieter community of Mesquite, just up the I-15 from Vegas, where many courses await you for some fine desert golf. Southern Nevada too hot for you? Reno might be for you, in Northern Nevada, where you can still get your gambling fix, but play golf among the pines or the beautiful high desert country. In the future, look for Coyote Springs, about 45 miles Northeast of Las Vegas for some Jack Nicklaus resorts.
